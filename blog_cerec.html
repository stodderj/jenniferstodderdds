Cerec and CAD/CAM in Dentistry

April 25, 2023

CEREC (Chairside Economical Restoration of Esthetic Ceramics) is a technology that uses milling devices and CAD/CAM (computer-aided design and computer-aided manufacturing) technology to create custom-made dental crowns in a single appointment.

The process starts with a digital scan of the patient's teeth, which is then used to create a 3D model of the tooth that is to be restored. A dentist uses CAD/CAM software to design the crown, taking into account the shape, size, and color of the tooth, as well as the patient's bite and smile. Once the design is complete, the digital model of the crown is sent to a CEREC milling device, which creates the final restoration from a block of ceramic material.

One of the main advantages of CEREC technology is that it allows dentists to create custom-made dental crowns in a single appointment, eliminating the need for multiple visits and temporary crowns. This can save patients time and money, and can also help to reduce the risk of complications such as tooth sensitivity or infection.

CEREC technology also allows for the creation of highly precise and accurate dental restorations that are custom-fit to the patient's teeth. The use of CAD/CAM technology ensures that the crowns are designed to match the patient's natural teeth in terms of shape, size, and color, which can help to improve the overall appearance of the restoration.

In summary, CEREC technology uses milling devices and CAD/CAM technology to create custom-made dental crowns in a single appointment. This can save patients time and money, and can also help to reduce the risk of complications such as tooth sensitivity or infection.
